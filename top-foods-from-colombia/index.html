Top Foods from Colombia

April 13, 2015

Colombians aren’t very keen on vegetables as most main courses consist of lots of meats, beans, and plantains, often fried. However, fresh fruits and fruit smoothies also are very versatile and plentiful. Colombians like to mix two polar flavors together making for some exciting combinations such as the areas ice cream with casquitos jelly and the hot chocolate with cheese.

The Caribbean part of Colombia offered some great grilled seafood, which was a refreshing break from all the red meats. Another favorite from the Caribbean part were the filling yuca chips. Plantains chips or pancakes were included in almost all main dishes as they are cheap and grow abundantly in the region. The arepas were one of our favorite snacks, in particular the ones from Juan Valdez Café.

We also indulged in a variety of spirits: anything ranging from beers, rum, canelazo, and Aguardiente were generally quite cheap. However, the one thing that we ate throughout our stay in Colombia was the delicious ice cream from Crepes & Waffles, rotating different funky fruit flavors.

  • The best: coffee with arepa on the side,
  • The worst: fried trout & pork,
  • The healthiest: the coffee, as sugar is not required.
April 2, 2015 • Poker beer - popular in Bogotá (our favorite).
April 6, 2015 • Araza fruit ice cream (very similar to passion fruit in taste) with casquitos; a jelly made from the outer part of the coffee cherry fruit (very nice sweet and sour taste).
April 6, 2015 • Amazing Arabica coffee.
April 8, 2015 • Choclomelos: chocolate covered marshmallows, not as good as the German version.
April 13, 2015 • Colombian rum - spicy flavors and quite smooth.
April 2, 2015 • Ajiaco: a potato-based soup with corn and capers, topped with sour cream.
April 2, 2015 • Bandeja Paisa: a heavy plate consisting of chorizo sausages, grilled steak, fried pork rind, rice, and beans. Topped with a fried egg, avocado, and sweet banana chips. It’s considered the national dish of Colombia. We found one was plenty for two people to share, however it was a bit too heavy for us.
April 2, 2015 • Hot chocolate (non-sweetened) with cheese - the cheese did not alter the taste of the hot chocolate drastically, however it was an interesting taste.
April 2, 2015 • Maracuya coconut cocada: super sweet but the sour passion fruit taste balanced the sugar and coconut very well.
April 2, 2015 • Aguardiente: anise liquor that is super cheap and popular in Colombia (we didn’t like it because of the strong anise taste as it resembled the green fairy quite a bit).
April 2, 2015 • Obleas: thin wafer with condensed milk in the middle.
April 3, 2015 • Arepa cornmeal pancake with gooey cheese in the middle (yummy, the corn version not so yummy).
April 5, 2015 • Corn Arepa with cheese.
April 3, 2015 • Aguilar beer - we preferred the regular one instead of the light version.
April 3, 2015 • French fries sprinkled with paprika, delicious.
April 3, 2015 • Delicious ice cream from Crepes & Waffles, close in quality and price to Argentinian ice cream.
April 4, 2015 • Costeña - a beer we found in Pereira - not good.
April 5, 2015 • Canelazo, a hot cinnamon drink with rum - yum!
April 5, 2015 • Fried trout with plantain chip - tasteless and not good for you, we would recommend spending a little extra for the grilled version.
April 7, 2015 • Cordilleras Artisan beer - quite tasteful compared to most Colombian beers.
April 11, 2015 • Club Colombia, the most popular beer in Colombia, but not our favorite.
April 12, 2015 • Grilled bass with patroness (green plantains made into thick pancakes) with Arroz con Coco (rice cooked in coconut milk, sugar, and raisins). We were quite disappointed with the rice as the raisins overpowered the dish. The fish, on the other hand, was very well prepared.
April 12, 2015 • Coconut and lime lemonade - we did not like the mix of the two flavors.
April 12, 2015 • Water in a bag? How does that work?
April 13, 2015 • Ron Viejo de Caldas - local spicy Colombian rum.
